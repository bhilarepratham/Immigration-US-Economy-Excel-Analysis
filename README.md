# Immigration and the US Economy: A Comprehensive Statistical Analysis (1980-2021)

## üìå Project Overview

This project presents a **data-driven statistical analysis** examining the relationship between **immigration and key U.S. macroeconomic indicators** over a 42-year period (1980‚Äì2021). The analysis was conducted **entirely in Microsoft Excel**, leveraging built-in analytical tools to derive evidence-based insights relevant to economics, policy, and business decision-making.

The study evaluates whether immigration has a statistically significant impact on:

* GDP Growth
* Unemployment Rate
* Personal Income Growth

## üîÑ Methodology

1. **Data Collection & Integration**

   * Combined 42 years of immigration, economic, and labor market data (1980‚Äì2021)

2. **Data Cleaning & Preparation**

   * Handled missing values in growth and lagged variables
   * Verified data integrity and removed duplicates
   * Identified and documented outliers (e.g., 2008‚Äì09 recession, COVID-19)

3. **Feature Engineering**

   * Net Immigration
   * GDP Growth Rate
   * Personal Income Growth
   * Lagged Immigration & GDP variables

4. **Statistical Analysis**

   * Correlation analysis to identify linear relationships
   * Time-series visualization of long-term trends
   * Multiple Linear Regression using Excel‚Äôs Data Analysis ToolPak

5. **Model Validation**

   * Train‚ÄìTest Split:

     * Training: 1980‚Äì2015
     * Testing: 2016‚Äì2021
   * Evaluated using R-squared, p-values, residual plots, and MAPE

---

## üîç Key Findings

* Immigration was **not a statistically significant predictor** in any of the three models
* GDP and labor-market fundamentals were the **primary drivers** of economic performance
* Economic cycles (recessions, recoveries) showed **little direct correlation** with immigration flows
* Results align with existing economic literature on the limited short-run macroeconomic effects of immigration
